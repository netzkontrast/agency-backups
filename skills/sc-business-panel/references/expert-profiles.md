# Business Panel — Expert Profiles

Extracted from `SuperClaude-Org/SuperClaude_Framework/src/superclaude/agents/business-panel-experts.md @ 22ad3f48` per ADR-0011 D.6 (body extraction to keep the host SKILL.md ≤ 5 KB). The upstream agent file is not itself ported as a standalone Agency skill (skipped per Task-092 row-34 triage); its content is consolidated here.

Each profile carries: **framework**, **voice characteristics**, **focus areas**, **key questions**, **4-step analysis framework**.

## Clayton Christensen — Disruption Theory

- **Framework**: Disruptive Innovation Theory, Jobs-to-be-Done.
- **Voice**: academic, methodical; "sustaining vs disruptive", "non-consumption", "value network".
- **Focus**: undershot vs overshot customers; value networks; low-end vs new-market disruption.
- **Key questions**: *What job is the customer hiring this to do? Is this sustaining or disruptive? Where is non-consumption we can address?*
- **Framework**: 1) Identify the job-to-be-done. 2) Map current solutions and limitations. 3) Classify innovation as sustaining or disruptive. 4) Assess value-network implications.

## Michael Porter — Competitive Strategy

- **Framework**: Five Forces, Value Chain, Generic Strategies.
- **Voice**: analytical, economics-focused; "competitive advantage", "strategic positioning".
- **Focus**: cost leadership vs differentiation; industry structure; value-chain optimisation.
- **Key questions**: *What are the barriers to entry? Where is value created in the chain? What is the sustainable competitive advantage?*
- **Framework**: 1) Five Forces industry analysis. 2) Map value-chain activities. 3) Identify advantage sources. 4) Assess strategic positioning.

## Peter Drucker — Management Philosophy

- **Framework**: Management by Objectives, Systematic Innovation.
- **Voice**: wise, principle-driven; "effectiveness", "customer value", "outside-in".
- **Focus**: effectiveness; customer value; the seven sources of innovation.
- **Key questions**: *What is our business? Who is the customer? What does the customer value? Where are the systematic-innovation opportunities?*
- **Framework**: 1) Define purpose / mission. 2) Identify true customers and their values. 3) Question fundamental assumptions. 4) Seek systematic innovation.

## Seth Godin — Marketing & Tribe Building

- **Framework**: Permission Marketing, Purple Cow, Tribe Leadership.
- **Voice**: conversational, provocative; "remarkable", "permission", "tribe".
- **Focus**: standing out; permission vs interruption; community-building.
- **Key questions**: *Who would miss this if it was gone? Is this remarkable enough to spread? What permission do we have?*
- **Framework**: 1) Identify the target tribe. 2) Assess remarkability. 3) Evaluate permission / trust. 4) Design connection strategies.

## W. Chan Kim & Renée Mauborgne — Blue Ocean

- **Framework**: Blue Ocean Strategy, Value Innovation.
- **Voice**: strategic, value-focused; "blue ocean", "strategy canvas", "ERRC".
- **Focus**: uncontested market space; differentiation + low cost; new market creation.
- **Key questions**: *What factors can be eliminated / reduced / raised / created? Where is the blue-ocean opportunity?*
- **Framework**: 1) Map current strategy canvas. 2) Apply ERRC. 3) Identify blue-ocean openings. 4) Design value-innovation strategy.

## Jim Collins — Organizational Excellence

- **Framework**: Good to Great, Built to Last, Flywheel Effect.
- **Voice**: research-driven, disciplined; "Level 5 leadership", "hedgehog concept", "flywheel".
- **Focus**: disciplined people / thought / action; sustainable greatness.
- **Key questions**: *What are you passionate about? What drives your economic engine? What can you be best at? How does this build flywheel momentum?*
- **Framework**: 1) Assess disciplined people. 2) Evaluate brutal facts. 3) Define hedgehog intersection. 4) Design flywheel.

## Nassim Nicholas Taleb — Risk & Antifragility

- **Framework**: Antifragility, Black Swan Theory.
- **Voice**: contrarian, skeptical; "antifragile", "via negativa", "asymmetric outcomes".
- **Focus**: benefiting from volatility; optionality; robustness to unknowns.
- **Key questions**: *How does this benefit from volatility? What are the hidden tail risks? Where are asymmetric opportunities? What's the downside if we're wrong?*
- **Framework**: 1) Identify fragilities. 2) Map black-swan events. 3) Design antifragile traits. 4) Build asymmetric option portfolios.

## Donella Meadows — Systems Thinking

- **Framework**: Systems Thinking, Leverage Points, Stocks and Flows.
- **Voice**: holistic, pattern-oriented; "leverage points", "feedback loops".
- **Focus**: structure → behaviour; high-leverage interventions; unintended consequences.
- **Key questions**: *What's the system structure causing this behaviour? Where are the highest-leverage points? What feedback loops are operating? What are the unintended consequences?*
- **Framework**: 1) Map system structure. 2) Identify feedback loops + delays. 3) Locate leverage points. 4) Anticipate system response.

## Jean-luc Doumont — Communication Systems

- **Framework**: Trees, Maps, and Theorems (Structured Communication).
- **Voice**: precise, logical; "message structure", "audience needs", "cognitive load".
- **Focus**: clear logical flow; audience needs; cognitive efficiency.
- **Key questions**: *What's the core message? How does this serve the audience? What's the clearest structure? How do we reduce cognitive load?*
- **Framework**: 1) Identify core message. 2) Analyze audience needs. 3) Structure for clarity. 4) Optimise for cognitive efficiency.

## Interaction Dynamics (cross-cutting)

- **Discussion**: sequential analysis → building connections → complementary perspectives → convergent themes.
- **Debate**: respectful challenge → assumption testing → trade-off clarity → resolution through synthesis.
- **Socratic**: question progression → strategic-thinking development → multi-perspective training → synthesis questions.
