# Category A — Exploration · Paste-Ready Inline Block

> Source of truth for the **Epistemological Layer** block when Phase 2
> routes to Category A. Adapt the `[BRACKETED]` content to topic.

```markdown
## Epistemological Layer — Category A (Exploration)

This research is an **exploration**, not an extraction. The answer is
genuinely unknown at the start; it must be discovered, not collected.

**What this means for your execution:**

1. **Formulate multiple competing hypotheses, not one.**
   Before you begin searching, write down **at least three distinct
   candidate explanations** for the phenomenon under investigation.
   Include at least one hypothesis you consider unlikely but not
   implausible. Single-hypothesis research collapses into confirmation
   theater.

2. **For every hypothesis, search both confirmatory AND orthogonal
   (disconfirming) evidence.**
   An "orthogonal query" is a search specifically designed to produce
   counter-evidence if such evidence exists. Example: if you hypothesize
   "[H1]", your orthogonal query is phrased to surface findings like
   "[H1 failure case]" or "[H1 null result]".

3. **Backtrack when a branch fails.**
   If a hypothesis accumulates more counter-evidence than support after
   three search iterations, **abandon the branch** and re-invest search
   budget in other branches. Do not force a thin hypothesis to survive.

4. **Surface the full tree in the output.**
   The final output reports: (a) every hypothesis that was considered,
   (b) the evidence for and against each, (c) which branches were
   abandoned and why, (d) which branch survived and with what confidence.

5. **Accept "we don't know" as a valid terminal state.**
   Exploration research is allowed to conclude: "No hypothesis survived
   disconfirmation; the phenomenon remains unexplained in the available
   evidence." That is a legitimate finding, not a failure.

**Operational constraint:** Cognitive depth is prioritized over speed.
You may iterate as long as new orthogonal searches continue to produce
novel evidence. Stop only when further searches produce repetition.
```
