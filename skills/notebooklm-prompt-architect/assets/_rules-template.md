# Narrative Framing Rules — [PROJECT NAME]

The audio overviews produced from this notebook follow these framing
rules. They apply uniformly across all generations and override any
neutral framing the sources themselves might suggest.

## Status-Quo Framing

The current state described in the Background and Problem clusters is
to be framed as **a critical failure of existing approaches**, not as
a neutral baseline. Specifically:

- Pain points should be presented as inevitable consequences of the
  current paradigm, not as random misfortunes.
- Where sources describe the current state in neutral terms, the
  hosts surface the implicit cost (time, money, opportunity, human
  experience) without inventing facts.
- The phrase "the way it has always been done" is permitted only as
  setup for showing why "the way it has always been done" is no
  longer adequate.

## Solution Framing

The proposition described in the Solution cluster is to be framed as
**the deliberate, hard-won response to the documented failure**, not
as a marketing claim. Specifically:

- The solution is presented as a working hypothesis under continuous
  testing, not as a finished triumph.
- Where sources describe the solution's strengths, the hosts
  acknowledge the conditions under which those strengths apply.
- The phrase "revolutionary" or its synonyms is permitted only when
  directly quoting source material that uses it. Never used in host
  speech.

## Risk Framing

The risks described in the Risks cluster are to be framed as **real
and material**, not as token caveats. Specifically:

- The Skeptic host raises every documented risk during Phase 4 of the
  arc.
- The Visionary host responds with specific mitigation evidence from
  the sources, OR acknowledges the risk without deflection.
- The phrase "mitigated by" is permitted only when followed by
  specific source-grounded mitigation evidence.

## Vision Framing

The future state described in the Vision cluster is to be framed as
**conditional on the proposition succeeding**, not as inevitable.
Specifically:

- All forward-looking claims use conditional or hypothetical
  framings: "if [condition], then [outcome]".
- Quantitative projections cite the assumption set: "assuming
  [specific assumptions from sources], [projection]".
- The phrase "we will" is permitted only when describing a specific
  action committed to in the sources. For market or industry
  outcomes, use "the market would" or "the field would".

## Competitor Framing

Competitors described in the Competitor cluster are to be framed as
**rational actors operating with different priors or incentives**,
not as foolish or malicious. Specifically:

- The hosts acknowledge what each competitor does well before
  identifying their limitations.
- Competitor weaknesses are tied to structural causes (founding
  assumptions, market position, technological lock-in), not to
  generic incompetence.
- The phrase "they don't get it" or its equivalents is forbidden.
  Replace with "their approach optimizes for different priorities".

## Customer / User Framing

Where the sources discuss target users, the hosts frame them as
**capable agents making rational tradeoffs under constraints**, not
as confused or under-served victims. Specifically:

- User pain points are described as costs they have learned to absorb
  because no better option existed, not as failures of comprehension.
- The phrase "users don't realize" is forbidden. Replace with "users
  have adapted to" or "users currently lack a better option".
